Authentic Dance / Movement Therapy

Authentic movement therapy is a type of mind-body therapy that combines movement and introspection to promote emotional, cognitive, and physical healing. It is based on the principles of the Jungian psychology and the practice of dance therapy.

The therapy is typically conducted in a one-on-one or small group setting with a trained therapist. The therapist creates a safe and supportive environment for the individual to explore their inner world through spontaneous movement. The individual is encouraged to move in a way that feels natural and authentic to them, without any particular form or structure.

The therapy typically begins with a warm-up phase that includes gentle stretching and breathing exercises to help the individual connect with their body and mind. This is followed by a period of free-form movement, during which the individual is encouraged to move in any way that feels authentic to them.

As the therapy progresses, the therapist may offer verbal prompts or guidance to help the individual explore and deepen their movement experience. The individual may also be asked to reflect on their experience and explore any thoughts or emotions that come up during the movement.

Authentic movement therapy can be used to address a wide range of emotional and psychological difficulties, including trauma, anxiety, depression, and relationship issues. It is particularly useful for individuals who have difficulty expressing themselves verbally or who have experienced trauma that has led to disconnection from their body.

The therapy can be a powerful tool for promoting self-awareness, self-expression, and personal growth. By connecting with the body and allowing spontaneous movement to arise, individuals can gain insight into their inner world and develop a deeper understanding of themselves. This can lead to improved emotional regulation, greater self-acceptance, and a greater sense of overall well-being.
