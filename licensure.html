Licensure Supervision

BC-DMT (Board Certified Dance/Movement Therapist) licensure supervision is a professional process in which a qualified and experienced BC-DMT provides guidance and support to a therapist who is seeking to become licensed in the field of dance/movement therapy. This supervision helps the therapist gain the necessary skills and competencies to practice dance/movement therapy in a safe, ethical, and effective manner, and prepares them for the BC-DMT licensure exam. BC-DMT licensure supervision is a crucial step in the development of dance/movement therapists and helps to ensure that clients receive high-quality care.

Upon completion of the BC-DMT licensure supervision, trainees are eligible to apply for licensure as a Board-Certified Dance/Movement Therapist. The licensure process includes passing a written examination and meeting other requirements established by the ADTA.

Overall, BC-DMT licensure supervision is an essential component of the training and licensure process for individuals seeking to become Board-Certified Dance/Movement Therapists. It provides trainees with the opportunity to gain practical experience and develop their clinical skills under the guidance of a qualified supervisor, ensuring that they meet the standards of competency and ethical practice established by the ADTA.
